Stage 1: Traditional Operations

Manual processes, paper-based systems, reactive service

Guest Data Management

Preference cards, spreadsheets, staff memory. Information lives in silos and rarely transfers between departments.

Reservation System

Phone calls, email confirmations, manual calendar management. Double bookings happen, information gets lost.

Guest Communication

Generic emails, printed materials, reactive responses. No personalization, no automation, no consistency.

Service Delivery

Staff asks same questions multiple times. Preferences aren't shared across departments. Service feels disjointed.

Common Pain Points

  • Guests repeat preferences to multiple staff members
  • Staff can't access guest history quickly during interactions
  • No visibility into guest behavior or spending patterns
  • Manual processes create opportunities for errors
  • Limited ability to personalize experiences at scale
  • Reactive service—always responding, never anticipating

Stage 2: Basic Digital

Multiple disconnected systems, some automation, limited integration

Guest Data Management

Reservation System

Guest Communication

Common Pain Points

  • Integration between systems requires constant maintenance
  • Data syncs lag, creating outdated information
